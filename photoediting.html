Imperfect Transmittance

This is my year 1 end project for a Digital Photography & Imaging course-an attempt to make a collage using multiple pictures and edit.

My background concept story of this art is that illusion is a proof that you don't always see what you think you do-because of the way your brain and your entire visual system perceive and interpret an image. For instance, visual illusions happened due to properties of the visual areas of the brain as they receive and process information. In other words, your perception of an illusion has more to do with how your brain works -- and less to do with the optics of your eye. Therefore I believe that, everything that we perceive is an illusion.

Software used : Adobe Photoshop
